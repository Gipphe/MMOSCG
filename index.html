Educating children in Syria

The Syrian conflict is disrupting the education of millions of children, in addition to threatening their physical safety and psychosocial well-being. According to the Save the Children report, “The Cost of War” (2015), approximately 2.8 million Syrian children are not receiving any form of education inside Syria or across the region. Literacy rates were at 95% for 15-24 year-olds prior to the start of the conflict, but Syria now has one of the lowest school enrolment rates in the world.

Achieving reading and writing fluency (literacy) is foundational for lifelong learning, and therefore a particularly important skill for all children to acquire. Being literate in the language one uses at home also makes it easier to learn second languages and hence facilitates the integration into host-country education systems.
